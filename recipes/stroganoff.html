Brazilian Stroganoff

Brazilian stroganoff, also known as "Estrogonofe de Carne" in Portuguese, is a popular dish in Brazil that has its roots in the classic Russian beef stroganoff but has been adapted to suit Brazilian tastes. It's a hearty and creamy dish typically made with tender strips of beef, onions, mushrooms, and sometimes diced tomatoes, all cooked in a creamy sauce made with ketchup, mustard, and heavy cream.

The Brazilian version often includes white rice as a side dish, and it's not uncommon to find it served with shoestring potatoes or potato sticks on top for added texture and flavor. Some variations may also include ham or bacon for extra richness. It's a comforting and flavorful meal that is commonly enjoyed in Brazilian households and restaurants alike.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef (sirloin or tenderloin), thinly sliced into strips
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste or ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup beef broth or water
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or butter
  • White rice, for serving
  • Shoestring potatoes or potato sticks, for garnish (optional)
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook Beef and Vegetables:
    2. Brown sliced beef in oil, then set aside. Saut√© onion, mushrooms, and garlic until softened.
  2. Make Sauce:
    3. Stir in tomato paste, Dijon mustard, and beef broth. Simmer until slightly thickened.
  3. Combine Ingredients:
    4. Return cooked beef to the skillet. Pour in heavy cream, season with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve:
    5. Simmer for a few more minutes. Serve hot over cooked white rice. Garnish with shoestring potatoes and parsley if desired.
